📋 How to Use This Roadmap

This progression roadmap is organized into 5 gear score stages. Each stage shows exactly what you need — bosses to kill, weapons to craft, blood types to use, and materials to farm. Follow the stages in order. Each stage builds on the last, and skipping ahead means fighting enemies that will one-shot you.

The Golden Rule of Progression: Never fight a boss more than 5 gear score below the recommendation. At a 10+ gear score deficit, most bosses will one-shot you. When in doubt, upgrade your gear first — the roadmap below shows you exactly how.

The timeline below visualizes the exact order. Each stage card includes:

  • Gear Score Range — when to start and when to move on
  • Critical Bosses — the must-kill bosses at this stage (with links to the full boss order)
  • Recommended Weapons — what to craft and use at this tier
  • Best Blood Type — which blood to prioritize for this stage
  • Key Unlocks — what new crafting and abilities open up

❓ Progression FAQ

Do I need to kill every boss to progress?
No. Only about 15 of the 64 V Blood bosses are required to reach Dracula. These are the bosses that unlock weapon tiers, armor recipes, or critical abilities (movement forms, spell slots, crafting stations). This roadmap highlights exactly which ones. The remaining 49 bosses provide spells, cosmetics, and side-grade recipes — valuable but optional. See the full boss order for the complete list.
What's the fastest way to increase gear score?
Gear score is the average of your weapon and armor levels. The fastest way to increase it: (1) Always craft the highest-tier weapon available first — weapons contribute more to GS than individual armor pieces, (2) Upgrade your weapon at the Smithy/Improved Smithy for +2 to +4 bonus GS, (3) Don't neglect armor — a full set of the current tier's armor is worth 5-8 GS points. Craft weapons first, armor second, upgrades third. Weapon tier list →
When should I start the Oakveil content?
The Invaders of Oakveil expansion content is designed for GS 70+ players. Start exploring Oakveil after you've crafted your first Sanguine weapon and have full Dark Silver or Immaculate Armor (GS 68-75). Oakveil introduces new weapon types (Twinblade, Claws, Throwing Daggers), new Mutant blood type, and new bosses. It's parallel endgame content — you can do Oakveil before or after Dracula.
