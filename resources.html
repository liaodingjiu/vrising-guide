📋 How to Use This Farming Guide

V Rising has 5 resource tiers that gate your progression. Each tier requires materials from specific biomes. This guide organizes every material by tier — follow the gear score ranges to know when and where to farm each resource.

Golden Rule: Always use Worker blood (60%+ quality) when doing dedicated farming runs. Worker blood gives a 20-30% bonus to all resource yields — it turns a 100-ore run into a 120-130 ore run, saving you hours across a full playthrough.

Each tier section below includes a material table with exact farming locations, followed by optimization tips for maximizing your yield per minute. The material tables also show what each resource is used for and link to related guides — so you know not just where to farm, but why you're farming it.

Farming Tip: The Haunted Iron Mine (southwest Dunley) is the single best Iron farming spot in the game. Bring an Iron Pickaxe — one hit yields 3-6 ore. A full mine clear takes ~10 minutes and yields 100-150 Iron Ore. Build a small castle outpost near the mine entrance to stash ore and respawn quickly if you die to the mine's skeleton guards.
Pro Tip: After defeating Quincey the Bandit King (GS 27), prioritize crafting Iron weapons BEFORE Iron armor. A stronger weapon kills enemies faster, which means you farm ore faster. Armor can wait — DPS is the best farming tool in V Rising.

⚡ Farming Optimization Tips

Seven tips that turn inefficient farming into a streamlined operation. Apply these and cut your material grind time by 40-60%.

1. Worker Blood is Non-Negotiable

60%+ Worker blood gives 20-30% bonus resource yield on every node. On a 150-ore Iron Mine run, that's 180-195 ore instead of 150. Over a full playthrough, Worker blood saves 10-15 hours of farming. Keep a high-quality Worker prisoner in your castle at all times. Full blood type guide →

2. Build Farming Outposts

Place a small castle (2x2 rooms minimum) near major farming zones — one near the Haunted Iron Mine, one near eastern Silverlight. Use these as stash points and respawn locations. The 10-minute walk back to a farming zone after dying is the biggest hidden time-sink in V Rising. Castle building guide →

3. Use the Right Tool

Mace/Pickaxe for mining nodes (highest yield per hit). Axe for wood (fastest swing). Sword for combat farming (wide arc for clearing packs). Don't mine ore with a Sword — you get less per node. Weapon tier list →

4. Farm at Night, Craft at Day

Silverlight Hills and Gloomrot have daylight penalties. Farm these zones during in-game nighttime. During daytime, stay in your castle and process your haul — smelt ores, craft planks, organize chests. This rhythm maximizes efficiency and minimizes sun-death frustration.

5. Servant Missions Are Passive Income

A full castle of 6-8 servants running 2-hour resource missions generates 200-400 of each basic material while you're offline. Set up your servant economy early and always have missions running. Servant guide →

6. Use Waygates for Multi-Zone Runs

Plan farming routes that hit multiple zones using Waygate teleports. Example: start in Farbane for Copper → Waygate to Dunley for Iron Ore → Waygate to Cursed Forest for Scourgestone. Three resources in one in-game night cycle.

7. Horse = Mobile Storage

Your horse has a small inventory. Load it with heavy materials (ore, stone, wood) during farming runs. Park it safely outside a node cluster, fill your inventory, dump into horse, repeat. Horse inventory + player inventory doubles your haul per trip. Beginner guide →

☢️ Radium Alloy — Where to Farm, Recipe & How to Unlock

Radium Alloy is one of the most frustrating late-game bottlenecks. I spent 3 hours running around Gloomrot killing random mobs and got 2 drops. Then I found the real farming route — and got 8 in 20 minutes.

What Is Radium Alloy Used For?

Radium Alloy is a Sanguine-tier crafting material used for endgame weapons and armor upgrades. You need roughly 12-16 Radium Alloy to fully upgrade one weapon to max tier. If you're running two weapons (say, Spear + Longbow), double that number.

Where to Find Radium Alloy (Best Farm Route)

The most reliable source is Gloomrot factory enemies — specifically the Technomancers and Power Core Guardians in the industrial zone. They have a roughly 15-20% drop chance per kill. But the real farm is the generators:

  • Gloomrot Power Plant (north sector): 3 generators with guaranteed Radium Alloy spawns nearby. Clear the area once and you'll walk out with 3-5. Respawns in ~15 minutes.
  • Gloomrot Factory (east sector): Technomancers drop Radium Alloy at roughly 1 in 5 kills. Take a circular route through the factory floor — start at the east entrance, loop through the assembly line, exit north. 5-8 Alloy per clear.
  • Gloomrot Labs (underground): Higher drop chance but harder enemies. Only worth it at GS 85+.
The route I use every new playthrough: Start at north Power Plant (3-5 Alloy from generators) → ride southeast to Factory (5-8 from Technomancer loop) → if I'm feeling greedy, Labs underground (2-4 more). Total: ~20 minutes, 10-17 Radium Alloy. If you're below GS 80, skip the Labs — Factory + Power Plant alone is enough.

Radium Alloy Recipe & How to Unlock

The crafting recipe unlocks after defeating Voltatia the Power Master (GS 86) in Gloomrot. Once unlocked, you can craft Radium Alloy at the Advanced Furnace (not the regular Furnace — make sure you've upgraded).

Crafting Recipe:

  • 3× Tech Scrap (farmed from Gloomrot machines and Technomancers)
  • 1× Power Core (rare drop from Power Core Guardians, or crafted at the Power Station)
  • 1× Sanguine Ore (mined in Gloomrot — see Sanguine farming section above)
If you can't unlock the recipe yet (pre-GS 86): You can get Radium Alloy without crafting it. Send servants on Gloomrot missions — they'll bring back 1-3 per run. Build a servant team with GS 70+ gear and run Gloomrot missions overnight. This is how I got my first Radium Alloy weapons before I could even survive the Factory.

❓ Resource Farming FAQ

What's the most efficient early-game farming route?
For GS 0-20: Start at Farbane Woods graveyard → clear skeletons for Bones (5 min) → sweep wolf dens for Animal Hide (3 min) → mine copper nodes along western cliffs (8 min) → clear bandit camp for Whetstones (5 min). Total: ~20 minutes for a full early-game resource haul. Repeat 2-3 times to fully gear up in Bone → Copper equipment.
Do resource nodes respawn?
Yes, all resource nodes in V Rising respawn on a timer. Ore nodes respawn roughly every 10-15 minutes of in-game time. Tree nodes respawn faster (~5-8 minutes). The respawn timer is per-node and independent of other nodes — a node starts its respawn timer the moment you finish mining it. Server settings can adjust respawn rates (private servers often have faster respawns).
How do I get Sanguine materials before GS 80?
You can't safely farm Sanguine Ore before GS 80 — Gloomrot enemies will kill you in 1-2 hits. However, you CAN get Sanguine materials before GS 80 through: (1) Killing Foulrot the Soultaker (GS 55) for the Sanguine weapon recipe, (2) Sending high-gear-score servants on Gloomrot missions (they can bring back Sanguine materials), (3) Trading with other players on PvP servers. The intended path is to reach GS 80+ through Dark Silver gear first, then enter Gloomrot.
Where to get Radium Alloy in V Rising?
The best Radium Alloy farm is the Gloomrot Power Plant (north sector) — 3 generators with guaranteed spawns nearby, 3-5 Alloy per clear. Follow up with the Factory east sector — Technomancers drop them at ~20% rate, circular loop gives 5-8 per clear. If you can't survive Gloomrot yet, send GS 70+ servants on Gloomrot missions — they bring back 1-3 Radium Alloy per run. Full farming route →
How to craft Radium Alloy in V Rising?
Recipe: 3× Tech Scrap + 1× Power Core + 1× Sanguine Ore, crafted at the Advanced Furnace. Unlocked after defeating Voltatia the Power Master (GS 86) in Gloomrot. Before GS 86, you cannot craft Radium Alloy — you must farm the Power Plant and Factory for direct drops, or use servants.
